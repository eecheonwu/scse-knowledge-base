# SCSE Body of Knowledge

## Overview

The **SCSE Body of Knowledge (SCSE-BoK)** is the central repository of concepts, theories, principles, methodologies, models, metrics, and terminology that define the **SSOT-Centric Software Engineering (SCSE)** research programme.

It serves as the authoritative reference for the intellectual contributions developed throughout the programme and provides a structured view of the knowledge that underpins SCSE.

Unlike traditional bodies of knowledge that document established practices within a discipline, the SCSE-BoK is a living and evolving knowledge base. It captures both the foundational concepts inherited from existing software engineering research and the original contributions emerging from the SCSE research programme.

The Body of Knowledge provides a common language for researchers, practitioners, educators, and AI software engineering agents while ensuring consistency across the framework, research publications, software prototypes, and future industrial applications.

---

# Purpose

The SCSE Body of Knowledge has four primary objectives.

- Document the concepts that define the SCSE research programme.
- Provide a structured reference for current and future research.
- Maintain consistency across all SCSE publications and software artifacts.
- Serve as the authoritative conceptual foundation for the SCSE ecosystem.

---

# Scope

The Body of Knowledge covers every major concept that contributes to the development of SSOT-Centric Software Engineering.

These concepts range from foundational software engineering principles to emerging ideas developed specifically within the SCSE research programme.

The knowledge captured within this repository continues to evolve alongside the research and will expand as new concepts are proposed, validated, and integrated into the framework.

- SSOT-Driven Software Engineering (SDSE)
- Knowledge-Centric Software Engineering
- Engineering Knowledge
- Engineering Intent
- Knowledge Continuity
- Semantic Consistency

# Core Concepts

The following concepts currently represent the core intellectual contributions of the SCSE research programme.

| Concept | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| SSOT-Centric Software Engineering (SCSE) | A knowledge-centric software engineering paradigm that positions the Single Source of Truth as the authoritative engineering knowledge system. |
| SSOT-Driven Software Engineering (SDSE) | A methodology that translates the knowledge contained within an SSOT into software engineering activities and deliverables. |
| Architecture Memory | A structured representation of architectural decisions, rationale, constraints, and design knowledge preserved throughout the software lifecycle. |
| Engineering Knowledge Ontology (EKO) | A semantic model for representing engineering concepts and their relationships. |
| SSOT Knowledge Graph | A graph-based representation of engineering knowledge supporting reasoning and traceability. |
| Knowledge Translation Theory | A conceptual model describing how engineering knowledge is transformed into software artifacts while preserving intent. |
| SSOT Translation Quality Evaluation (STQE) | A proposed framework for measuring the quality of translating engineering knowledge into implementation plans, task plans, source code, tests, and deployment artifacts. |
| SSOT-Driven Change Impact Analysis | A methodology for identifying the architectural and implementation impact of proposed software changes using the SSOT. |
| SSOT-Driven Feature Change and Addition | A structured process for evolving software systems while maintaining knowledge continuity and architectural integrity. |

# Relationship to the Canonical Research Library

The SCSE Body of Knowledge is built upon the Canonical Research Library.

The library provides the scientific and engineering foundations of the research programme by collecting and organizing existing knowledge from books, journal articles, conference papers, standards, and technical reports.

The Body of Knowledge extends that foundation by synthesizing existing knowledge, identifying research gaps, and introducing new concepts that collectively define SCSE.

Together, these two sections ensure that every original contribution can be traced back to the body of knowledge from which it emerged.
