# Canonical Research Library

## Overview

The Canonical Research Library is the curated collection of books, journal articles, conference papers, technical reports, standards, and other scholarly resources that provide the intellectual foundation for the **SSOT-Centric Software Engineering (SCSE)** research programme.

Rather than serving as a traditional bibliography, this library represents the body of existing knowledge from which SCSE draws its theoretical foundations, engineering principles, methodologies, and validation approaches.

Each resource included in this library has been intentionally selected because it contributes to one or more research themes within SCSE.

---

# Purpose

The Canonical Research Library serves several important objectives.

- Establish the scientific foundation of the SCSE research programme.
- Identify the existing state of knowledge within each research area.
- Support the development of new theories and methodologies.
- Provide traceable evidence for future publications.
- Guide empirical validation and experimental design.
- Maintain a structured body of knowledge that evolves alongside the research programme.

The library ensures that every significant concept introduced by SCSE is grounded in established research while clearly distinguishing original contributions from existing work.

---

# Organization of the Library

The library is organized into thematic domains that collectively support the development of SCSE.

## 1. Software Engineering Foundations

This collection provides the fundamental principles of software engineering, software processes, software quality, software maintenance, software evolution, and engineering best practices.

Representative topics include:

- Software Engineering
- Software Architecture
- Software Design
- Software Evolution
- Software Quality
- Software Maintenance

---

## 2. Requirements Engineering

Resources in this category focus on requirements elicitation, requirements analysis, specification, validation, and traceability.

These works provide the foundation for understanding how stakeholder intent can be preserved throughout the software lifecycle.

---

## 3. Software Architecture

This category explores architectural design, architectural decision making, architecture documentation, architecture governance, and architecture evaluation.

It forms an important foundation for the Architecture Memory concepts developed within SCSE.

---

## 4. Architecture Knowledge Management

These resources investigate how architectural knowledge is created, captured, shared, maintained, and reused throughout software development.

This area directly influences the SCSE vision of preserving engineering knowledge as a first-class engineering asset.

---

## 5. Knowledge Engineering

Knowledge Engineering provides methods for representing, organizing, reasoning about, and maintaining structured knowledge.

Topics include:

- Knowledge Representation
- Knowledge Management
- Semantic Technologies
- Knowledge Graphs
- Knowledge-Based Systems

---

## 6. Ontology Engineering

Ontology Engineering contributes techniques for formally representing engineering concepts, relationships, constraints, and domain semantics.

These principles influence the development of the Engineering Knowledge Ontology (EKO) proposed within SCSE.

---

## 7. Knowledge Graphs

Knowledge Graph research provides techniques for modeling engineering knowledge as interconnected semantic networks.

Within SCSE, Knowledge Graphs are investigated as an enabling technology for AI-assisted software engineering.

---

## 8. Artificial Intelligence

This section focuses on Artificial Intelligence methods applicable to software engineering, including:

- Machine Learning
- Generative AI
- Large Language Models
- Intelligent Decision Support
- AI Planning
- Autonomous Systems

---

## 9. AI-Native Software Engineering

This emerging research area investigates how AI systems participate directly in software development activities.

Topics include:

- AI Coding Assistants
- Autonomous Software Engineering
- Agentic Software Engineering
- AI-Augmented Development
- Multi-Agent Collaboration

---

## 10. Empirical Software Engineering

Empirical Software Engineering provides methodologies for evaluating software engineering techniques through experiments, case studies, industrial evaluations, and quantitative analysis.

These methods will support the validation of SCSE concepts and metrics.

# Research Strategy

The Canonical Research Library is continuously maintained throughout the research programme.

New resources are added according to the following criteria.

- Relevance to one or more SCSE research themes.
- Scientific quality and credibility.
- Influence within the software engineering community.
- Potential contribution to future SCSE concepts.
- Support for empirical validation.
- Applicability to AI-native software engineering.

This approach ensures that the library remains both comprehensive and focused.
