# SCSE Research Monograph

## Overview

The **SCSE Research Monograph** is the primary scholarly publication of the **SSOT-Centric Software Engineering (SCSE)** research programme.

The monograph consolidates the theories, methodologies, frameworks, empirical findings, and practical applications developed throughout the research programme into a single comprehensive reference. It serves as the definitive publication describing the evolution of SCSE from its foundational concepts to its practical application in AI-native software engineering.

While individual journal papers and conference publications present specific contributions, the monograph provides the complete narrative that connects these contributions into a coherent body of knowledge.

---

# Purpose

The SCSE Research Monograph serves to:

- Document the complete SCSE research programme.
- Present the theoretical foundations of SSOT-Centric Software Engineering.
- Introduce new methodologies developed throughout the research.
- Demonstrate how the individual research contributions relate to one another.
- Provide a long-term reference for researchers, practitioners, educators, and students.
- Establish SCSE as a coherent software engineering framework.

# Relationship to Publications

The monograph and the publication strategy are closely connected.

Individual journal papers, conference papers, technical reports, and white papers investigate specific aspects of SCSE in depth. Their findings are later synthesized and incorporated into the monograph.

Conversely, new chapters developed within the monograph may identify research questions that lead to future publications.

This iterative relationship ensures that both the monograph and the publication portfolio evolve together throughout the research programme.
